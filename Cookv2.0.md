SECTION 4 — PROFESSIONAL CRYSTALLIZATION PROCESS

Step 1: Pre-Dissolution Preparation
	•	Clean all glassware with DI water and ethanol, allow to dry.
	•	Weigh powders individually using the analytical scale.

Step 2: Dissolving Powders
	1.	Combine all powders in a beaker.
	2.	Add hot distilled water (~50°C) in small increments (~12 mL).
	3.	Stir continuously with a magnetic stirrer or rod.
	4.	Optional: Use ultrasonic bath to remove stubborn particles.
	5.	Monitor solution clarity; it must be fully clear—no floating particles.

Step 3: Filtration
	•	Filter solution through medium-grade filter paper or coffee filter.
	•	Repeat if necessary to remove micro-impurities (key for shard clarity).

Step 4: Controlled Crystallization
	1.	Transfer the filtered solution into a clean crystallization dish.
	2.	Cover loosely with a watch glass to prevent dust but allow slow evaporation.
	3.	Place in a stable, vibration-free environment at 20–25°C.
	4.	Optional: Use a humidity-controlled chamber (<40% RH) to reduce cloudiness.
	5.	Evaporation Timeline:
	•	Rapid initial evaporation → formation of seed crystals
	•	Slow final evaporation (~72–120 hours) → gem-like, larger shards

Step 5: Crystal Harvesting
	•	Once fully dry (solution fully evaporated, no wet patches):
	•	Gently remove crystal mass using a spatula
	•	Break into shards or leave as clusters
	•	Optional: Place in desiccator for 12–24h to ensure dryness
